# Attribution and Compliance

## What this skill is

`gnu-free-software` is an independent skill that helps produce and audit software,
documentation, and projects so they qualify as **free software** in the sense
defined by the Free Software Foundation and conform to the GNU Coding Standards and
GNU philosophy.

## Relationship to the source material

The conventions, definitions, and terminology this skill applies originate with FSF
and GNU Project documents, credited in `CREDITS.md`. This skill **summarizes and
restates that guidance in its own words and structure**. It does **not** reproduce,
mirror, or redistribute the source documents, and it is **not** a modified version
or derivative *distribution* of them.

This distinction matters: several of the source documents are licensed
**CC BY-ND 4.0** (Attribution-NoDerivatives), which permits sharing the work
verbatim with attribution but not distributing modified versions. Because this
skill conveys the underlying facts, policies, and conventions in original prose
rather than copying or altering those texts, it stays within those terms. The
**GNU Coding Standards** is licensed under the **GFDL**, which does permit
derivatives; even so, the material here is an original restatement, credited to its
source.

If you wish to read or redistribute the originals themselves, use the canonical
URLs in `CREDITS.md` — that is the authoritative text, kept current by the FSF.
